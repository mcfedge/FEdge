
## 1. Segmentation: How Should You Segment Federal CISOs?

**A. By Department/Industry**

- **Civilian Agencies:** (e.g., Department of State, Department of Education, GSA, EPA)
    
- **Defense & Intelligence:** (e.g., DoD, NSA, DIA)
    
- **Health & Human Services:** (e.g., HHS, CDC, NIH)
    
- **Financial/Regulatory:** (e.g., Treasury, SEC, FDIC)
    
- **Law Enforcement & Justice:** (e.g., DOJ, FBI, DHS)
    
- **Independent Agencies & Commissions:** (e.g., NASA, FTC, FCC, FEC)
    

**B. By Agency Size or Mission Criticality**

- Large, high-profile agencies (DoD, DHS, HHS, Treasury)
    
- Medium/specialized agencies (NASA, GSA, EPA)
    
- Small/independent commissions (FEC, NMB, USAC)
    

---

## 2. Prioritization: How to Prioritize Within Segments

**A. Risk Tolerance**

- Agencies with recent breaches or public cybersecurity incidents (higher urgency, likely more open to new solutions)
    
- Agencies with critical infrastructure or sensitive data (higher risk, more regulatory pressure)
    

**B. Technology Adoption Curve**

- Agencies with active modernization/cloud initiatives (early adopters, more open to innovation)
    
- Agencies with legacy systems or slow procurement (laggards, harder to penetrate)
    

**C. Strategic Value**

- Agencies aligned with your solution’s strengths (e.g., if you offer cloud security, target agencies with cloud migration projects)
